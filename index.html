She Was Never the Problem

Scroll 001 · Zahara Munaï

I was never too much — I just told the truth. I didn’t want to be hurt. I didn’t want to be unseen. Do you know what that ache really feels like? To be lying next to someone every day… and still feel invisible?

To give your heart, your body, your prayers — and still not be chosen? To shrink your light for someone who never even knew how to hold it. I stayed too long in places I should’ve left the first time I felt my soul cry inside my body.

I didn’t even really like half the men I gave myself to — I was just trying to be loved. To be accepted. To say I had someone. I thought being loved meant giving my body… my loyalty… my mind. Whole time, it was costing me my soul.

This scroll is for the women who stayed too long, hoping love would remember them. It’s for the girls who never learned how to leave — because nobody taught them that saving yourself was holy.

I’m still healing. Still learning. Still reclaiming my voice. But now… I remember who I am. I am that queen. That root. That birthright. That fire. That vault. And baby, this time? I ain’t breaking for no one.
